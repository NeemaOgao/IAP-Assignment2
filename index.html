ROSA PARKS

Previous Address: 43201-3203 Virginia Park Street in Detroit, Michigan

Biography

Rosa Louise McCauley Parks, (1913-2005), was an African American civil right’s activist whom the U.S. Congress dubbed the “Mother of the Modern-Day Civil Rights Movement”. Sheis one of the most enduring symbols of the tumultuous civil rights era of the mid-twentieth century. Her 1955 arrest in Montgomery for refusing to give up her bus seat to a white man sparked the Montgomery Bus Boycott and set in motion a chain of events that resulted in ground-breaking civil rights legislation and helped to bring Martin Luther King Jr. to the forefront as the movement’s leader.

Education

She attended local rural schools, and after the age of 11, the Industrial School for Girls in Montgomery. However, she later had to opt out of school to look after her grandmother.

Employment History

Rosa Parks was a seamstress Parks and later on became an administrative aide in the Detroit office of Congressman John Conyers Jr. in 1965, a post she held until her 1988 retirement2. In 1987, she co-founded the Rosa and Raymond Parks Institute for Self-Development, to serve Detroit’s youth.

Awards and distinctions
  1. In 1979, the NAACP bestowed upon Rosa Parks the Spingarn Medal in recognition of her heroic actions during the Montgomery bus boycott1.
  2. In 1996, she was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom from President Bill Clinton.
  3. In 1997, she was awarded the Congressional Gold Medal – the highest award of Congress.
  4. In 1998, received the International Freedom Conductor award.
  5. She was selected to be one of the people to meet Nelson Mandela on his release from prison in 1994.
